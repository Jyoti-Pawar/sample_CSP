Internet Of Things

IoT is short for Internet of Things. The Internet of Things refers to the fast-evolving network of physical objects with internet connectivity, and the communication between these objects and other Internet-enabled devices and systems. The Internet of Things is the extension of internet connectivity beyond computers, laptops, smart phones to a wide range of everyday objects that use embedded technology for communication to interact with the surrounding environment. Various organizations use IoT for efficient customer service and improved decision making to surge the value of their business.

Global Skill Gap

Research from Immersat research program reveals that 33 percent of organizations would benefit from additional skills, while 47 percent say they lack IoT skills altogether. According to Zebra Technologies’ inaugural Intelligent Enterprise Index, 62 percent of companies plan to deploy IoT initiatives company-wide in the future and 42 percent of enterprises are spending at least $3.1 million annually on IoT. So, there’s a time crunch to ramp up training and education to catch up to the demand for the needed skills.
